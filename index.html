Parang Batik is one of the oldest batik motifs in Indonesia. Parang comes from the word Pereng which means slope. Perengan depicts a diagonal line descending from high to low. The arrangement of the S motifs intertwining unbroken symbolizes continuity.

This batik is an identical work of batik art and has even become an icon of batik in Cirebon and other parts of Indonesia. This batik motif has a uniqueness that is not found in other batik-producing areas. Even because it is only in Cirebon and is a masterpiece, the Ministry of Culture and Tourism will register the Megamendung motif to UNESCO to get recognition as one of the world heritages.

This batik is a batik motif whose shape is in the form of a circle similar to a kawung fruit that is neatly arranged geometrically. Sometimes, this motif is also interpreted as an image of a lotus flower with four petals that are open. Lotus is a flower that symbolizes longevity and purity. This batik motif means perfection, purity and holiness.

Simbut batik has the shape of a leaf that resembles a taro leaf. This motif is the simplest motif, only compiling and tidying up one type of motif. The Simbut motif comes from the inland Bedouin tribe in Sunda which is thick with old civilizations. However, over time, the Bedouin residents who accepted modernity developed this batik in the coastal areas of Banten. So that Simbut batik motif is also known as Banten batik.

Gentong batik is a batik that is soaked in color by using a barrel. The batik industry is located in Paseseh Village, Tanjungbumi District, Bangkalan Madura Regency. In the past, this batik was made to fill the spare time of fishermen's wives who were waiting for their husbands to come home from sea.

Tujuh rupa batik is one of the batik motifs that adds to the many treasures of batik in Indonesia. For those of you who don't know, this batik comes from Pekalongan, a coastal area in Central Java. Various batik motifs are spread across Indonesia, and each batik owned has its own cash characteristics.
